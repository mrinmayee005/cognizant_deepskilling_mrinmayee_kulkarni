Text Alignment

Left-aligned text: This is the default alignment for most text. It creates a clean left edge, making it easy to read for languages that read left to right.

Center-aligned text: Often used for headings, titles, and short paragraphs that need visual emphasis.

Right-aligned text: Sometimes used for dates, captions, or metadata alongside content.

Justified text: This paragraph is justified, meaning it stretches to fill the full width of the container. The text aligns evenly on both the left and right edges, creating a polished, newspaper-like appearance.
